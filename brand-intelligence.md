## Typography decision

Public CSS declares:

- Space Grotesk for display typography;
- IBM Plex Sans for body typography;
- IBM Plex Mono for utility and data labels.

All three families are open-source and are loaded through Google Fonts with licensed system fallbacks. No proprietary font files are copied or committed.

## Public visual grammar

Axial Search's current public identity is high-contrast and precise: near-black green fields, pale typography, vivid mint rules, restrained yellow accents, geometric display type, practical sans-serif body copy, and mono utility labels. The candidate campaign translates that grammar into an editorial executive brief rather than copying Axial's navigation or site layout.

## Imagery decision

No company photography is used. The role and firm are better represented by the official wordmark and an original Fractional AI Mandate Charter derived from Axial's mandate-first search philosophy. This avoids generic executive stock photography and keeps the visual argument centered on the work.

## Artifact continuity

The website, resume, cover letter, interview thesis brief, 120-day plan, fit analysis, and mandate charter share the official logo, documented tokens, type hierarchy, dark identity bands, mint rules, mono evidence labels, and six-clause operating vocabulary.
