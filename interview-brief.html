Interview thesis brief · Independent candidate vision

Turn AI ambition into an operating mandate.

Russell Dudek's point of view on the work behind a Fractional Chief AI Officer engagement, the tensions that must be resolved, and the operating assets that should remain after the engagement.

Executive premise

A fractional CAIO earns leverage by making the permanent mandate clearer, not by becoming a permanent dependency.

What Axial is really matching

Not a generic title. A combination of mandate, business context, technical burden, governance exposure, change capacity, and the client's readiness to grant real authority.

What the client is buying

Senior judgment at the moment when AI ambition is larger than operating clarity: which work matters, what must be controlled, who must own it, and what should become permanent.

Mandate tensions

What makes fractional AI leadership difficult
Russell Dudekhttps://russelldudek.github.io/Axial/
The engagement is valuable because the contradictions cannot be delegated to a technical team or solved by a list of use cases.

Fractional authority / enterprise accountability

The leader must influence board and C-suite decisions without relying on title, tenure, or a large permanent organization. Decision rights and sponsorship must be explicit.

Speed / governance

Pilots need momentum, but uncontrolled experimentation creates shadow AI, unclear data rights, weak evaluation, and no path to scale. Portfolio and controls should be designed together.

Portable method / specific mandate

A reusable diagnostic accelerates discovery. The answer must still change with the industry, operating model, risk, economics, data, technical estate, and adoption burden.

Executive strategy / workflow reality

Board confidence matters, but value is realized where roles, handoffs, judgment, rework, cycle time, and customer consequences actually change.

Technical possibility / investment discipline

The question is not whether a model or vendor can demonstrate capability. It is whether the organization should own, buy, partner, integrate, govern, and sustain it.

Interim leadership / permanent capability

A strong engagement must transfer cadence, decision records, talent priorities, governance, and evidence into a permanent owner or operating structure.

Cost of leaving the tension unresolved

Portfolio sprawl

Demand expands while ownership and evidence remain weak.

Governance drag

Controls arrive late and are perceived as blockers rather than design inputs.

Adoption theater

Licenses, demos, and training are mistaken for changed work and durable value.

Scorecard categories

Decision quality

Priority clarity, decision latency, named authority, and scale/stop discipline.

Operating evidence

Cycle time, rework, completeness, exceptions, reliability, customer or employee consequences.

Trust and control

Governance coverage, evaluation, exception closure, human authority, and traceability.

Adoption

Workflow use, repetition, role clarity, support demand, and feedback closure.

Economics

Cost to build and run, avoided effort, revenue or margin path, and investment options.

Handoff readiness

Owner capability, cadence independence, talent plan, documentation, and open decisions.
