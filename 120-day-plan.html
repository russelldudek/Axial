120-day entry plan · Fractional Chief AI Officer

Diagnose fast. Decide deliberately. Transfer from the beginning.

An illustrative operating plan designed to be recalibrated to the client's mandate, sector, risk, technical estate, and executive capacity.

The first 120 days should create a board-governable mandate, a small evidence-bearing portfolio, and an operating system that can transfer to permanent ownership.

Principles

Mandate before volume

Do not expand the use-case list until outcomes, authority, portfolio logic, and guardrails are explicit.

Work before theater

Attach AI to real workflow decisions, roles, handoffs, economics, and consequences.

Evidence before scale

Define what will justify scale, redesign, hold, or stop before the pilot becomes politically permanent.

Governance in the design

Human authority, data, evaluation, fallback, vendor accountability, and exception handling are part of the product.

Adoption has an owner

Training is not adoption. Name the workflow owner, behavior change, reinforcement, and feedback mechanism.

Handoff starts on day one

Build cadence, records, talent, and decision rights so progress can survive the engagement.

Days 31-60 · Choose the portfolio

Prioritize

  • Value and strategic relevance
  • Workflow owner and adoption burden
  • Data and technical readiness
  • Risk and human authority
  • Reuse and learning potential

Decide technical posture

  • Build, buy, partner, or defer
  • Agent, assist, automate, or keep human-led
  • Vendor accountability and exit conditions
  • Evaluation, cost, latency, fallback
  • Integration and ownership boundaries

Install governance

  • Acceptable use and access
  • Data and source controls
  • Human review and release authority
  • Exceptions and escalation
  • Monitoring and decision records

Mobilize owners

  • Executive sponsor
  • Business and workflow owner
  • Technical and data owner
  • Risk, legal, security, finance
  • Adoption and enablement owner

Days 91-120 · Transfer the system

Confirm the permanent mandate

Clarify whether AI remains a dedicated executive mandate, moves into technology/product/operations, or requires a permanent CAIO search.

Transfer decision cadence

Internal owners run portfolio review, governance exceptions, value evidence, technical choices, adoption issues, and board reporting.

Set the talent sequence

Define executive, product, engineering, data, governance, enablement, and operating roles in the order the portfolio needs them.

Make the engagement decision

Extend for a bounded next mandate, convert to permanent leadership, transfer to the owner, or close because the system is stable.

Final 120-day readout: What are we trying to change? What have we chosen not to do? Can we govern the portfolio? Is adoption becoming durable? Who owns the mandate next? Each answer must point to a named owner and operating evidence, not a future aspiration.
