01 / The real work

The title is portable. The mandate is not.

A fractional AI executive enters when the organization needs senior judgment before it has enough clarity, confidence, or permanent capacity. The job is to make the brief more precise while producing enough operating value to justify what comes next.

Fractional authority
VS
Enterprise accountability
Influence must become explicit decision rights, sponsorship, and escalation paths rather than personality-dependent access.
Speed to proof
VS
Governance from day one
The fastest pilot is not necessarily the fastest path to durable adoption. Guardrails and value evidence should be installed together.
Portable method
VS
Client-specific judgment
A repeatable diagnostic is useful. A recycled answer is not. The operating model must change with the mandate, risk, economics, and adoption burden.
Interim leadership
VS
Permanent capability
A successful fractional engagement makes itself less necessary by transferring decision systems, evidence, talent, and cadence.

Make the mandate governable before making the portfolio bigger.

AI strategy fails quietly when ambition expands faster than ownership, evidence, and workflow change. The fractional CAIO's leverage is not a longer list of use cases. It is a sharper set of decisions.

01

A board-ready mandate

Business outcome, decision rights, human authority, risk posture, and investment logic are explicit enough to govern.

02

Each initiative has an owner, workflow, benefit hypothesis, technical path, adoption plan, and scale/stop condition.

03

A transfer-ready operating system

Cadence, decision records, talent plan, governance, and learning survive the fractional executive's departure.

02 / The operating artifact

Six clauses convert an ambiguous brief into an executable engagement.

The charter is deliberately not a maturity score. It is a decision contract: what outcome matters, who has authority, which work gets funded, what controls apply, how adoption is owned, and what makes the handoff real.

01

Outcome

Define the operating or market consequence worth changing.

Decision
What problem is strategically worth executive attention?
Evidence
Baseline, unit, beneficiary, and leading/lagging measures.
02

Authority

Make sponsorship, ownership, human judgment, and escalation visible.

Decision
Who can fund, stop, release, override, and accept risk?
Evidence
Named roles, decision record, and exception path.
03

Portfolio

Choose a small set of bets with different time horizons and proof burdens.

Decision
Build, buy, partner, sequence, scale, hold, or stop?
Evidence
Value, feasibility, data, reuse, cost, and capacity.
04

Guardrails

Attach governance to the work instead of placing it in a separate appendix.

Decision
What must be true before use, release, or expansion?
Evidence
Access, source, evaluation, fallback, monitoring, and audit trail.
05

Adoption

Specify what people will do differently and who owns the behavior change.

Decision
Where does AI alter work, role, handoff, or judgment?
Evidence
Usage, repetition, cycle time, rework, trust, and feedback.
06

Continuity

Define the permanent operating destination before the engagement ends.

Decision
Extend, transfer, hire, reorganize, or retire the mandate?
Evidence
Owner readiness, cadence, talent, documentation, and unresolved risk.

05 / First 120 days

Diagnose fast. Decide deliberately. Transfer from the beginning.

The plan assumes a fractional engagement measured in executive decisions and organizational movement, not the volume of meetings or slides. Each phase produces a usable asset that survives into the next.

DAYS 1-30

Bound the mandate

Read the system before prescribing it.

  • Executive and workflow discovery
  • AI inventory and shadow-use scan
  • Decision-rights map
  • Value, risk, data, and adoption baseline
  • Draft mandate charter
DAYS 31-60

Choose the portfolio

Turn demand into explicit investment choices.

  • Prioritize a small portfolio
  • Define build/buy/partner posture
  • Install governance and exception paths
  • Name business and technical owners
  • Establish evidence standards
DAYS 61-90

Prove the operating model

Make one or two workflows demonstrate the whole system.

  • Mobilize cross-functional work
  • Instrument adoption and value
  • Stress technical and human controls
  • Review scale/hold/stop decisions
  • Coach internal owners
DAYS 91-120

Transfer the system

Leave durable capability, not dependency.

  • Confirm permanent mandate
  • Refine talent and hiring sequence
  • Transfer cadence and decision records
  • Set board reporting
  • Recommend extend, handoff, or close
